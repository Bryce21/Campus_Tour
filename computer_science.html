    Computer Science Course Requirements

  • Students take courses for Computer Science as well as Mathematics. Computer Science courses include: Programming I, Programming II, User Interfaces, Introduction to Computer Technology, Technical Support, Algorithm Design, Database I, Database II, Data Structures, Multimedia & Internet, Information Systems, Operating Systems, Integration Project, Oracle Technologies, Web Programming, Telecommunications, UNIX Networking, Object-Oriented Programming, and Windows Networking. Mathematics course include: Mathematics I and Mathematics II. At least one elective is also required. Students will also need to complete a final project during their senior year during which they collaborate with other students to prepare for typical work in their chosen career.

    Computer Science Club

  • Computer Science Club aims to help students understand up-to-date computer technology. The club reviews current hardware and software products, explores different areas of interest, and create a social environment where students with various technology experiences come together and share information with one another. The club provides technology resources for all students to advance their desired projects. The Computer Science Club has held a programming contest, built a robot, and researched artificial intelligence. Future events can be seen below for members to RSVP. The club meets weekly in the Department of Computer Science.
