Mathematics at Awesome University

Earning a Degree in Mathematics

  • Is a math degree right for you? 21% of mathematicians hold a Bachelors degree, 38% hold a Masters, and 33% hold a doctoral. Could you be part of this number? With your degree in mathematics, you can start your career in agent based modeling, computational science, cryptography, vulnerability analyses, image science, simulation modeling engineer, and much more. Common coursework for a math degree includes Algebra, Number Theory, Analysis, Functional Analysis, Applied Math, Statistics, Computational Mathematics, Mathematical Statistics, Mathematics, Geometry, Geometric Analysis, and Topology and Foundations.

    Math Tracking Sheet

  • There are different courses for different Math Tracks. Developmental Math courses include Basic Arithmetic, Intro to Algebra, and Intermediate Algebra. There is the STEM track which includes courses like College Algebra, Plane Trigonometry, Precalculus, Calculus I, Calculus II, Discrete Math, Linear Algebra, Differential Equations, and Calculus III. The Business Track requires College Algebra, Brief Calculus, and Mathematical Analysis for Business. The Education track requires Math for Elementary Teachers I and II, and a CLM score of 32+. All these tracks require the Developmental Maths Courses as pre-requisites.

    Math Club

  • Our mission is to encourage and support the study of mathematics, research, and friendship. The Math Club gathers weekly in the Math Department and also hosts weekend activities. The Math Club prepares a 3-student team to compete in the annual MAA NCS competition. Preparation for the competition includes bi-weekly problem solving sessions. Math Club also hosts various presenters to give talks on intriguing topics and relative math problems from the industry. Other events include Pi Day celebrations during which the club serves pie to interested students and faculty, and Fibonacci Bowling where scoring is done by adding the two previous scores.
